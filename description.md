Careers Page

Norfolk Patient Transport is always interested in hearing from qualified individuals who are passionate about patient care and community service. We are looking for reliable and compassionate professionals to join our team providing non-emergency medical transportation throughout Norfolk and Haldimand County.

Minimum Qualifications

Applicants must possess the following qualifications:

• Valid Basic Life Support (BLS) certification or CPR Level HCP
• Valid Ontario G Class Driver’s License
• Ability to remain calm and professional in emergency or stressful situations
• Ability to safely lift 250+ lbs using a stretcher or stair chair with a partner
• Strong verbal and written communication skills in English
• Safe driving record with a clean driver’s abstract
• Ability to obtain a Criminal Record Check with Vulnerable Sector Screening

Health & Immunization Requirements

Applicants must be able to provide documentation of up-to-date immunizations including:

• MMR
• DPT
• Polio
• Varicella
• Hepatitis B
• COVID-19 (minimum two doses)
• TB Skin Test (Step 1 within the last 12 months)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is non-emergency patient transport?
Non-emergency patient transport is transportation for individuals who need assistance getting to or from medical appointments but do not require an ambulance.

Who uses patient transport services?
Hospitals, retirement homes, dialysis clinics, and private patients commonly use these services.

Do you provide wheelchair transportation?
Yes, our vehicles are equipped to safely transport wheelchair patients.

Do you provide stretcher transportation?
Yes, we provide stretcher transport for patients who cannot sit upright or require additional assistance.

Do you offer hospital discharge transportation?
Yes, we regularly transport patients home after hospital discharge.

Can a family member ride with the patient?
In many cases a caregiver or family member may accompany the patient during transport.

Do you provide oxygen during transport?
If oxygen is required during transport, please mention it during booking so the proper equipment can be arranged.

How do I book a transport?
Bookings can be made through the website booking form or by calling dispatch anytime.

Do you offer service after 7 PM?
Yes, local after-hours transport may be available depending on scheduling.

What areas do you serve?
We serve Norfolk County and Haldimand County including Simcoe, Port Dover, Delhi, Brantford, Hamilton, Hagersville, Tillsonburg and surrounding areas.

What forms of payment do you accept?
We accept credit cards, cash, and e-transfer. Hospitals and care facilities may qualify for Net 30 billing.

Do you operate 24 hours a day?
Scheduled transport services typically operate from 7:00 AM to 7:00 PM, but dispatch is available 24/7 and you can call anytime.

Is this an ambulance service?
No. Norfolk Patient Transport provides non-emergency transportation for patients who do not require emergency medical care.

Our Story
Norfolk Patient Transport was founded by Tony Nagrani and Stacy Nicole Barrett, both of whom have deep roots in Simcoe and Norfolk County.

Tony grew up in Simcoe, Ontario, where his father has served as a physician in the Norfolk County community for over 40 years. That lifelong exposure to healthcare shaped Tony’s understanding of patient care, professionalism, and the importance of reliable, local medical transportation services. Today, Tony is a Certified Transport Specialist (CTS) through NEMTAC, providing safe, professional non-emergency medical transportation throughout Simcoe, Norfolk County, and surrounding areas.

Stacy Nicole Barrett also grew up in Simcoe and is a trained Personal Support Worker (PSW), with hands-on experience supporting patients with dignity, compassion, and attention to detail. Her background ensures that every patient transport—whether wheelchair, stretcher, or ambulatory—is handled with care and professionalism.

Together, Tony and Stacy are returning home to Simcoe to raise their family and build Norfolk Patient Transport as a trusted, local provider of non-emergency medical transportation (NEMT) services in Norfolk County. Their mission is to support hospitals, long-term care homes, retirement residences, clinics, and families across Simcoe, Delhi, Port Dover, Waterford, and surrounding communities.

Norfolk Patient Transport specializes in:

Having seen firsthand the challenges patients face when transportation is delayed or unavailable in Norfolk County, Tony and Stacy are committed to providing a responsive, reliable, and local solution that healthcare providers and families can depend on.


At Norfolk Patient Transport, we believe in Caring Beyond The Ride — delivering safe, professional, and dependable non-emergency medical transportation across Simcoe, Norfolk County, and surrounding areas.
