Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about non-emergency patient transport.

Door-to-door, bed-to-bed, and facility transfer workflows
What is non-emergency patient transport?

Non-emergency patient transport is scheduled medical transportation for people who need mobility support but do not require an emergency ambulance response.

Who typically uses Norfolk Patient Transport?

Patients using medical appointments, hospital discharges, dialysis treatment, care home transfers, and other planned healthcare-related travel commonly use the service.

Can a family member or escort ride along?

Often yes. Escort availability depends on the vehicle setup, the patient’s mobility needs, and how much support is required during the trip.

How far in advance should I book?

When possible, book 24 to 48 hours in advance. Same-day requests may still be available depending on dispatch capacity and route availability.

What information do you need when I book?

Have the patient’s name, mobility type, pickup and destination details, preferred date and time, contact information, and any special handling notes ready.

How does the booking confirmation process work?

After a request is submitted, dispatch reviews the trip details, confirms the transport setup, and follows up with the booking contact to finalize the ride.

Do you provide wheelchair transport?

Yes. We support wheelchair-accessible transport with securement systems and mobility-aware loading and unloading.

Do you provide stretcher transport?

Yes. Stretcher-capable trips are available for patients who cannot safely remain upright during transport.

Can you accommodate oxygen or higher-support trips?

Trip requirements are reviewed by dispatch in advance so the appropriate vehicle and staffing arrangement can be assigned when possible.

What payment methods are accepted?

Private-pay bookings can request common methods such as credit card, cash, or e-transfer. Approved facilities may qualify for invoice-based billing.

Do hospitals and facilities get Net 30 billing?

Approved hospitals, clinics, retirement residences, and care facilities may qualify for Net 30 billing and account-based booking workflows.

What areas do you serve?

Coverage includes Norfolk County, Haldimand County, Simcoe, Port Dover, Delhi, Brantford, Hamilton, Hagersville, Tillsonburg, and surrounding areas.

What if I am not sure whether my destination is covered?

Call dispatch directly. If the route fits operational coverage, the team will confirm pickup timing and transport suitability.

Still need help?

Contact dispatch for immediate support with booking, scheduling, or facility coordination.
